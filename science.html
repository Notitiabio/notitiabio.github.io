Gut Microbiome

The composition of microorganisms living inside the gastrointestinal tract is known as the gut microbiome. The gut microbiome in each person weighs about 2kg and contains at least 1000 different species of bacteria. These bacteria help us break down our food and produce other molecules that interact with our body on a daily basis. The interaction between the bacteria and the human body is the key for understanding how the gut microbiome can affect our health.

"The complexity and density of bacteria in our gut is such that it can be called our microbial Amazon Jungle."

Short Chain Fatty Acids (SCFA’s)

Short chain fatty acids (SCFAs) are produced by bacterial fermentation of the dietary fibers we eat. SCFAs provide energy for colon cells, reduce inflammation, and regulate satiety. Reduced SCFA production has been associated with various diseases, such as type 2 diabetes and colon cancer.
