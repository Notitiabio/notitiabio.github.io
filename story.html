Humans are superorganisms

We are made up of our own genome, and our microbiome, which is the millions of microbes living on and inside our bodies. Bacterial cells outnumber our cells 10:1, and their genes outnumber ours 100:1. The microbiome is an integral part of the human body, performing metabolic functions that are vital to our health.

Thus, the human body is a communal group of human cells and microbes working together for the common good. We would not be able to live without the help of our little friends.

Our gut is as complex as the Amazon Jungle

The gut has the highest concentration of microbes out of all of the body parts in the human body. Within the 2 kg (4.4 lbs) of microbes inside the gut, there are more than 1000 species of bacteria that are either beneficial, neutral, or harmful to the body. The complexity and density of bacteria in the gut is such that it can be called our microbial Amazon Jungle.
