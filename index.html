Humans are superorganisms

We are made up of our own genome, and our microbiome, which is the millions of microbes living on and inside our bodies. Bacterial cells outnumber our cells 10:1, and their genes outnumber ours 100:1. The microbiome is an integral part of the human body, performing metabolic functions that are vital to our health.

Thus, the human body is a communal group of human cells and microbes working together for the common good. We would not be able to live without the help of our little friends.

The gut has the highest concentration of microbes out of all of the body parts in the human body. Within the 2 kg (4.4 lbs) of microbes inside the gut, there are more than 1000 species of bacteria that are either beneficial, neutral, or harmful to the body. The complexity and density of bacteria in the gut is such that it can be called our microbial Amazon Jungle.

Dr. Liping Zhao, the co-founder of Notitia, discovered in 2018 that a group of beneficial gut bacteria called the Foundation Guild(TM), is responsible for structuring a healthy gut microbiome and keeping it stable. These microbes are like the tall trees of the rain forest, and they are the foundation species of a healthy gut ecosystem. Foundation Guild bacteria produce metabolites that are crucial to human health.

Opposite to the Foundation Guild is a group of harmful bacteria called the pathogen guild. These dangerous bacteria are responsible for creating harmful toxins that can damage the body and aggravate diseases. The Foundation Guild and the pathogen guild are two armies that are always at war with each other , the outcome of this fight would dictate whether if our microbiome is healthy or diseased.

The army that wins is the one that you support, and Notitia has developed products and services that can promote health by protecting and/or restoring the Foundation Guild of the gut microbiome.
